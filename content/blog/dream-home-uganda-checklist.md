## Building Your Dream Home: The Essential Checklist

Building a home is one of the most significant investments you'll ever make. In Uganda's unique climate and regulatory environment, getting it right from the start can save you years of headaches and millions of shillings.

### 1. Site Selection Is Everything

Before you even think about floor plans, spend time on site selection. In Kampala's hilly terrain, the difference between a good plot and a great one can affect your entire build:

- **Soil testing** — Is the ground stable? Expansive clay soils are common in areas like Kololo and require deeper foundations
- **Access** — Can construction vehicles reach the site? This affects material costs significantly
- **Drainage** — Where does water flow during heavy rains?
- **Orientation** — East-west or north-south? This affects natural lighting and passive cooling

### 2. Budget for the Unexpected

A common rule in Uganda's construction industry: whatever timeline and budget you've set, add **20-30% contingency**. Material prices fluctuate, weather causes delays, and discoveries during excavation are inevitable.

### 3. Hire Professionals Early

We've seen too many homeowners who start with a "friend who draws" and end up with plans that can't pass the municipal approval process. **A registered architect costs less than fixing a badly designed house.**

### 4. Think About Climate

Uganda's tropical climate demands specific design choices:

- **Overhangs**: Protect walls from rain and reduce solar gain
- **Cross-ventilation**: Every room should have at least two openings for airflow
- **Material selection**: Avoid materials that absorb heat and release it at night
- **Rainwater management**: Gutters, downpipes, and drainage channels are non-negotiable

### 5. Plan for the Future

Design your home to evolve with your family. Can a study become a bedroom? Can the carport be enclosed? Is the roof structure strong enough for solar panels later?
